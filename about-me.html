About me

By the end of my studies, I decided that psychology is not what I want to do for a living. While I don't have a new precise plan yet, I began working in an administrative position for a large corporation. After some time, I joined an IT company, still as an administrative worker where I had the opportunity to see what it's like to work in this industry. I was responsible for managing the company's second-largest office, located in Warsaw. My work was appreciated, and I was soon promoted to the position of assistant to the company's CEO and management board. This gave me an excellent opportunity to closely observe how they run the business. I had the chance to cooperate with almost every department. However, there wouldn't be a web development company without developers. I became curious about programming and wanted to try it. So I did, and it just clicked! It's fascinating how something that initially appeared like a Sci-Fi code from the Matrix is now making sense. That's so satisfying to build something from scratch that was previously a mysterious black box. It's surprising how much pride and joy something as simple as fetching data from an API or creating a registration form can bring. So, I decided to change my career. I left my previous job to fully dedicate myself to learning programming and eventually became a frontend developer. With some help from my experienced programmer friends, I am studying documentations, taking courses, learning from tutorials, and finally, making some pet projects to practice the knowledge gained. I can't wait to get my first commercial experience as a developer.
